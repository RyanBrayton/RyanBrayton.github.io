Professional Summary

Ryan Brayton is a recent graduate from UTSA.

He has completed a successful internship at Southwest Research Institute as a Student Engineer during the Fall and Spring semesters of his last year in college. While employed, Mr. Brayton co-designed and fully implemented an interactive multi-level menued user interface from scratch using an LCD screen he connected to a PIC microcontroller. With grace and ingenuity, he RAM optimized the design by 50% using static elements made to look dynamic and interactive. During this process Ryan took initative in creating documentation outlining the functionality and structure of the menus.

Ryan also contributed greatly to testing flight software using python scripts to guide manual and automated testing of the C based software. These tests caught 15+ errors and even detected some hardware faults. He always brought these errors to the teams attention for correction.

Also during Ryan's last year at UTSA, he was privilaged to lead the UTSA chapter of the Eta Kappa Nu (HKN) ECE honor society. This lead to another successful year of eight 3 hour sessions of PCB soldering workships, which taught over 200 students how to put together there microcontroller kits. Concurrently, Ryan assumed the leadership of his senior design team, where he lead them to successful completion of a safe control system for an FSAE electric car.

Before this turn of events, Ryan left his job at Dunkin' Donuts and pursued the accomplishments mentioned. This sacrifice shows the go-getter attitude Ryan has to jump out of a place of comfort and into a field completely new in order to contribute to a community and culture of technological advances. During that summer after leaving, Ryan even initiated a research position w/ a professor and lead a summer camp.

Before this turning point, Ryan displayed a focus seen by his sucess in school. He always acheived high marks (3.77 GPA) even while working 30 hours/week and still made time to help fellow classmates.
